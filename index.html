The history of domestication of cats

Advances in archaeology and genetics have shown that the domestication of the cat occurred in the Near East around 7500 BC. It is commonly kept as a house pet and farm cat Valued by humans for companionship and its ability to kill vermin, the cat's retractable claws are adapted to killing small prey like mice and rats.

Characteristics of cats

It has a strong, flexible body, quick reflexes, and sharp teeth, and its night vision and sense of smell are well developed. It is a social species, but a solitary hunter
Cats communicate through hissing,growling,purring and meowing.it may also use body language to communicate.cats communicate for various reasons including to show happiness,express anger,solicit attentention and observe potential prey. additionally,when communicating with their human counterparts, they do so to obtain what they want such as food, water, and attention.

Importance of cats

Health benefits: Cats can improve your mental and physical health: Stress and anxiety relief: Petting or playing with a cat can help you feel better. A cat's purr can calm your nervous system and lower your blood pressure.
Cardiovascular health: Cat owners may have a lower risk of heart disease and stroke.
Allergies: Children who are exposed to cats early in life may be more likely to develop an immune system that helps them fight other allergens.
Companionship: Cats can provide unconditional love and companionship
